# What is Attention? 

At its core, attention is a **mechanism that lets a model decide what information matters most** when making a prediction.

When you read a sentence:
“The stock surged after the Fed announcement”
Your brain doesn’t weigh every word equally.  
You zoom in on:
- _“surged”_
- _“Fed announcement”_
That selective focus = **attention**
##### In machine learning terms
Attention assigns **weights** to different inputs:

$$[  
\text{Output} = \sum (\text{importance weight}) \times (\text{input})  
]$$

So the model learns:
- what to ignore ❌
- what to emphasize ✅

Financial markets are:
- noisy
- non-stationary
- regime-driven

Attention helps answer:
> “Which past events are relevant to the current market condition?”

# How Attention Decides What to Focus On
We now formalize attention as a mathematical operator.

#### 1. Problem Setup

We are given a sequence:

$$  
X = {x_1, x_2, \dots, x_T}, \quad x_t \in \mathbb{R}^d  
$$

Goal: for each time step $t$, compute a representation: $h_t$

$h_t$ should not depend only on $x_t$, but should incorporate **relevant information from other time steps**.

So we aim for:
$$  
h_t = \sum_{\tau=1}^{T} \alpha_{t,\tau} \cdot x_\tau  
$$
where:
- $\alpha_{t,\tau}$ = importance of time $\tau$ for time $t$



#### 2. Learnable Projections: Q, K, V

Instead of comparing raw inputs, we project them into three spaces:
$$  
q_t = W_Q x_t,\quad k_\tau = W_K x_\tau,\quad v_\tau = W_V x_\tau  
$$
where:
$$
Q =
\begin{bmatrix}
q_1 \\
q_2 \\
\vdots \\
q_T
\end{bmatrix},
\quad
K =
\begin{bmatrix}
k_1 \\
k_2 \\
\vdots \\
k_T
\end{bmatrix},
\quad
V =
\begin{bmatrix}
v_1 \\
v_2 \\
\vdots \\
v_T
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Here:
$$  
W_Q, W_K, W_V \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{att}} \times d}  
$$
- Query $q_t$: what the current timestep is looking for
- Key $k_\tau$: representation of past timestep for comparison
- Value $v_\tau$: information stored at timestep $\tau$


#### 3. Similarity Function

We compute similarity between time $t$ and $\tau$:
$$  
s_{t,\tau} = q_t^\top k_\tau  
$$
$$  
q_t^\top k_\tau = |q_t| |k_\tau| \cos(\theta)  
$$

- Large value → aligned → similar
- Small value → unrelated


#### 4. Scaling

We scale the dot product:
$$  
\tilde{s}_{t,\tau} = \frac{q_t^\top k_\tau}{\sqrt{d_{\text{att}}}}  
$$

Without scaling:
- variance of dot product grows with dimension
- softmax becomes extremely sharp


#### 5. Softmax Normalization

Convert scores into probabilities:

$$  
\alpha_{t,\tau} =  
\frac{\exp\left(\tilde{s}_{t,\tau}\right)}  
{\sum_{\tau'} \exp\left(\tilde{s}_{t,\tau'}\right)}  
$$

$$  
\alpha_{t,\tau} \ge 0, \quad \sum_{\tau} \alpha_{t,\tau} = 1  
$$

So for each $t$, ${\alpha_{t,\tau}}$ is a probability distribution over all timesteps.

#### 6. Output Computation

Final representation:
$$  
h_t = \sum_{\tau=1}^{T} \alpha_{t,\tau} v_\tau  
$$

$$  
\boxed{ 
h_t =  
\sum_{\tau=1}^{T}  
\text{softmax}\left(\frac{q_t^\top k_\tau}{\sqrt{d_{\text{att}}}}\right)  
v_\tau  
}
$$

Matrix Form :
$$  
\boxed{  
\text{Attention}(Q,K,V) =  
\text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_{\text{att}}}}\right)V  
}  
$$



At time $t$:
$$  
h_t = \sum_{\tau} \alpha_{t,\tau} v_\tau  
$$
means:
> construct today’s signal as a weighted combination of past regimes, where weights depend on similarity to current conditions

>
> We are given a time series with 3 timesteps:
> $$
> X =
> \begin{bmatrix}
> 1 & 0 \\

\end{bmatrix}

\begin{bmatrix}

\end{bmatrix}

$$  
S = QK^\top =  
\begin{bmatrix}

1 & 1 \\  
2 & 1  
\end{bmatrix}  
\begin{bmatrix}  
1 & 0 & 1 \\

$$
S_{1,1} = 1\cdot1 + 0\cdot1 = 1

$$
S_{1,2} = 1\cdot0 + 0\cdot1 = 0

$$
S_{1,3} = 1\cdot1 + 0\cdot2 = 1

S_{2,2} = 1\cdot0 + 1\cdot1 = 1

S_{2,3} = 1\cdot1 + 1\cdot2 = 3

##### Step 4: Softmax:

Softmax formula applied to each element:  
$$  
\alpha_i = \frac{e^{x_i}}{\sum_j e^{x_j}}  
$$

$$
$$
\alpha_2 = \frac{e^{0}}{e^{0.707} + e^{0} + e^{0.707}} = \frac{1}{5.06} \approx 0.198
$$
$$
\alpha_3 = \frac{e^{0.707}}{e^{0.707} + e^{0} + e^{0.707}} = \frac{2.03}{5.06} \approx 0.401
$$
$$
\text{Row 1} = [0.401,\ 0.198,\ 0.401]
$$

Row 2
$$
x = [1.414,\ 0.707,\ 2.121]
$$
$$
\alpha_1 = \frac{e^{1.414}}{e^{1.414} + e^{0.707} + e^{2.121}} = \frac{4.11}{4.11 + 2.03 + 8.34} = \frac{4.11}{14.48} \approx 0.284
$$
$$
\alpha_2 = \frac{e^{0.707}}{e^{1.414} + e^{0.707} + e^{2.121}} = \frac{2.03}{14.48} \approx 0.140
$$
$$
\alpha_3 = \frac{e^{2.121}}{e^{1.414} + e^{0.707} + e^{2.121}} = \frac{8.34}{14.48} \approx 0.576

$$

Row 3

>
> $$
> x_1 = [0.01,\ 0] \quad \text{(small return, no news)}
> $$
>
> $$
> x_2 = [0.02,\ 0] \quad \text{(uptrend continues)}
> $$
>
> $$
> x_3 = [-0.05,\ 1] \quad \text{(WAR news + crash)}
> $$
>
> $$
> x_4 = [-0.02,\ 0] \quad \text{(aftershock)}
> $$
>
> $$
> x_5 = [-0.04,\ 1] \quad \text{(WAR escalation)}
> $$
>
> Feature meaning
> - First value = return  
> - Second value = news signal (1 = war, 0 = none)
>
> Value transformation
> $$
> W_V =
> \begin{bmatrix}
> 1 & 0 \\
> 0 & 3
> \end{bmatrix}
> $$
>
> So:
> $$
> v_t = [\text{return},\ 3 \times \text{news}]
> $$
>

Compute:
$$h_5 = \sum_{\tau=1}^{5} \alpha_{5,\tau} v_\tau$$
At time 5, model compares:
$$x_5 = [-0.04,\ 1]$$
Similarity reasoning
 - $x_1, x_2$: no news → low similarity  
 - $x_3$: war + crash → HIGH similarity  
 - $x_4$: no news → moderate  
 - $x_5$: identical → highest  


Approx attention weights
$$\alpha_{5,:} \approx [0.05,\ 0.05,\ 0.35,\ 0.10,\ 0.45]$$


Compute values
$$v_1 = [0.01,\ 0]$$
$$v_2 = [0.02,\ 0]$$$$v_3 = [-0.05,\ 3]$$$$v_4 = [-0.02,\ 0]$$$$v_5 = [-0.04,\ 3]$$
Output:
$$h_5 = 0.05v_1 + 0.05v_2 + 0.35v_3 + 0.10v_4 + 0.45v_5$$Result $$h_5 \approx [-0.038,\ 2.4]$$

### Intuition
- Model ignores calm periods  
- Focuses on past war events  
- Builds a “war-aware market state”
