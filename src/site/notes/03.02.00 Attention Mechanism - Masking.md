# Masking in Attention

>Masking is a mechanism in attention that **restricts which positions a timestep is allowed to attend to** by modifying the similarity matrix before applying softmax.

---

# Why masking is needed

In standard attention:

$$  
h_t = \sum_{\tau=1}^{T} \alpha_{t,\tau} v_\tau  
$$

This allows every timestep $t$ to attend to **all** positions $\tau \in [1, T]$, including the future.

At time t=2t = 2t=2, the model can use:
- x3,x4,x5x_3, x_4, x_5x3​,x4​,x5​ → future information
So it becomes:
“predict the past using the future”

Which is **data leakage** 🚨

---

# What masking enforces

Masking imposes a constraint:

$$  
\tau \le t  
$$

So attention becomes:

$$  
h_t = \sum_{\tau=1}^{t} \alpha_{t,\tau} v_\tau  
$$

> Each timestep can only use **past and present information**, never the future

---
# How masking is applied mathematically

Start with similarity:

$$  
S = QK^\top  
$$

We modify $S$ before softmax:

$$  
S_{t,\tau} =  
\begin{cases}  
q_t^\top k_\tau & \text{if } \tau \le t \  
-\infty & \text{if } \tau > t  
\end{cases}  
$$

### Why use $-\infty$

Softmax is:

$$  
\alpha_{t,\tau} = \frac{e^{S_{t,\tau}}}{\sum_j e^{S_{t,j}}}  
$$

If $S_{t,\tau} = -\infty$, then:

$$  
e^{-\infty} = 0  
$$

So:

$$  
\alpha_{t,\tau} = 0  
$$

Future positions receive **zero attention weight**

For sequence length $T = 3$:

$$  
S_{\text{masked}} =  
\begin{bmatrix}  
s_{1,1} & -\infty & -\infty \  
s_{2,1} & s_{2,2} & -\infty \  
s_{3,1} & s_{3,2} & s_{3,3}  
\end{bmatrix}  
$$
### Effect after softmax

- Row $1$ attends only to timestep $1$
- Row $2$ attends to $1, 2$
- Row $3$ attends to $1, 2, 3$

---
# Implementation idea

In practice, we apply a mask matrix $M$:

$$  
S_{\text{masked}} = S + M  
$$

where:
- $M_{t,\tau} = 0$ if $\tau \le t$
- $M_{t,\tau} = -\infty$ if $\tau > t$


> [!EXAMPLE]
>
> We are given a time series with 3 timesteps:
> $$
> X =
> \begin{bmatrix}
> 1 & 0 \\
> 0 & 1 \\
> 1 & 1
> \end{bmatrix}
> $$
>
> We define projection matrices:
> $$
> W_Q =
> \begin{bmatrix}
> 1 & 0 \\
> 1 & 1
> \end{bmatrix}, \quad
> W_K =
> \begin{bmatrix}
> 1 & 1 \\
> 0 & 1
> \end{bmatrix}, \quad
> W_V =
> \begin{bmatrix}
> 1 & 0 \\
> 0 & 2
> \end{bmatrix}
> $$
> 
> Attention Dimension: $d_{\text{attn}} = 2$
>
> Compute the **final masked attention output matrix**


##### Step 1: Compute Q, K, V

$$
Q = X W_Q =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 \\
1 & 1 \\
2 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
K = X W_K =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 1 \\
0 & 1 \\
1 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
V = X W_V =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 \\
0 & 2 \\
1 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$
##### Step 2: Compute Similarity Matrix

Transpose K

$$
K^\top =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 1 \\
1 & 1 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Similarity Matrix

$$
S = QK^\top =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 1 \\
2 & 1 & 3 \\
3 & 1 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

##### Step 3: Apply Masking

Causal mask

$$
M =
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & -\infty & -\infty \\
0 & 0 & -\infty \\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
S_{\text{masked}} = S + M =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -\infty & -\infty \\
2 & 1 & -\infty \\
3 & 1 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

##### Step 4: Scaling

$$
\tilde{S} = \frac{S_{\text{masked}}}{\sqrt{2}}
$$

$$
\tilde{S} \approx
\begin{bmatrix}
0.707 & -\infty & -\infty \\
1.414 & 0.707 & -\infty \\
2.121 & 0.707 & 2.828
\end{bmatrix}
$$

##### Step 5: Softmax

Row 1

$$
[0.707,\ -\infty,\ -\infty] \rightarrow [1,\ 0,\ 0]
$$

Row 2

$$
[1.414,\ 0.707,\ -\infty]
$$

$$
[e^{1.414},\ e^{0.707},\ 0] = [4.11,\ 2.03,\ 0]
$$

$$
\text{Sum} = 6.14
$$

$$
[0.669,\ 0.331,\ 0]
$$

Row 3

$$
[2.121,\ 0.707,\ 2.828]
$$

$$
[8.34,\ 2.03,\ 16.93]
$$

$$
\text{Sum} = 27.30
$$

$$
[0.305,\ 0.074,\ 0.620]
$$

Final Attention Matrix

$$
A =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0.669 & 0.331 & 0 \\
0.305 & 0.074 & 0.620
\end{bmatrix}
$$

##### Step 6: Compute Output

$$
H = AV
$$

Row 1

$$
h_1 = [1,0]
$$

Row 2

$$
h_2 = 0.669[1,0] + 0.331[0,2]
$$

$$
= [0.669,\ 0.662]
$$

Row 3

$$
h_3 = 0.305[1,0] + 0.074[0,2] + 0.620[1,2]
$$

$$
= [0.925,\ 1.388]
$$

Final Output

$$
H =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 \\
0.669 & 0.662 \\
0.925 & 1.388
\end{bmatrix}
$$

# Implementation 

```python
import numpy as np

def softmax(x):
    x = x - np.max(x, axis=-1, keepdims=True)  # stability
    exp_x = np.exp(x)
    return exp_x / np.sum(exp_x, axis=-1, keepdims=True)

def masked_attention(X, W_Q, W_K, W_V):
    """
    X: (T, d)
    W_Q, W_K, W_V: (d, d_attn)
    """

    # Step 1: Projections
    Q = X @ W_Q
    K = X @ W_K
    V = X @ W_V

    # Step 2: Similarity
    S = Q @ K.T

    # Step 3: Scaling
    d_attn = Q.shape[1]
    S = S / np.sqrt(d_attn)

    # Step 4: Causal Mask
    T = S.shape[0]
    mask = np.triu(np.ones((T, T)), k=1)  # upper triangle
    S = S - 1e9 * mask                   # simulate -inf

    # Step 5: Softmax
    A = softmax(S)

    # Step 6: Output
    H = A @ V

    return H, A
```

#### PyTorch Version 

```python
import torch
import torch.nn.functional as F

def masked_attention_torch(X, W_Q, W_K, W_V):
    Q = X @ W_Q
    K = X @ W_K
    V = X @ W_V

    d_attn = Q.size(-1)

    S = Q @ K.transpose(-2, -1)
    S = S / torch.sqrt(torch.tensor(d_attn, dtype=torch.float32))

    T = S.size(-1)
    mask = torch.triu(torch.ones(T, T), diagonal=1)

    S = S.masked_fill(mask == 1, float('-inf'))

    A = F.softmax(S, dim=-1)
    H = A @ V

    return H, A
```
