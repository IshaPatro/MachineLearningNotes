# FSTGAT: Financial Spatio-Temporal Graph Attention Network

📄 Paper: [https://www.mdpi.com/2073-8994/17/8/1344](https://www.mdpi.com/2073-8994/17/8/1344)

FSTGAT models financial markets as a system where both **time dynamics** and **cross-asset relationships** matter. It combines a temporal module with a graph attention module to capture these two dimensions simultaneously.FSTGAT models financial markets as something richer than a single time series. Instead of asking “how does one stock evolve over time,” it asks:

> How do prices evolve when multiple stocks interact with each other over time?

To represent this, the data is not just a sequence anymore. It becomes:

$$  
X \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times T \times d}  
$$

where $x_{i,t}$ is the feature vector of stock $i$ at time $t$. Now each timestep contains an entire _market snapshot_, not just one asset.

The key idea is to treat the market as a graph. Each stock is a node, and connections between stocks represent relationships like industry similarity or learned correlations. Instead of fixing these relationships, the model learns them using attention:

$$  
\alpha_{i,j} = \text{importance of stock } j \text{ when predicting stock } i  
$$

This means the model dynamically decides which other stocks matter.


>[!NOTE]
>It says: 
>to understand stock $i$ today, don’t just look at its own past. Look at:
>- its past behavior
>- other stocks
>- and how those stocks behaved at relevant times
>  
>all weighted by attention.

What makes this powerful is that relationships are not fixed. On a normal day, a stock might attend mostly to its sector. During a crisis, attention can shift across the entire market. The model adapts automatically.

The output is a prediction for each stock at each time step:
$$  
\hat{y}_{i,t}  
$$
which could be a return, price, or direction.

The intuition is simple but deep:
> each prediction is built from a weighted combination of past market states, across both time and assets

Instead of learning isolated patterns, the model learns **how the market moves as a system**.


FSTGAT has two core engines working together:
- a **temporal module** (learns patterns over time)
- a **graph attention module** (learns relationships across stocks)

Both are attention-driven in spirit, but operate along different dimensions.

---

## Temporal Module (time dynamics)

The goal here is to model how each stock evolves over time while enforcing causality:

$$  
\tau \le t  
$$

Instead of standard attention, FSTGAT uses **causal temporal convolution**.

For stock $i$, input sequence:

$$  
x_{i,1}, x_{i,2}, \dots, x_{i,T}  
$$

The temporal representation is computed as:

$$  
h^{(temp)}_{i,t} = \sum_{k=0}^{K} w_k \cdot x_{i,t-k}  
$$

What this means
- $K$ = kernel size (how far back we look)
- $w_k$ = learned weights
- only past values are used

#### With dilation 
To capture long-term dependencies:
$$  
h^{(temp)}_{i,t} = \sum_{k=0}^{K} w_k \cdot x_{i,t - d \cdot k}  
$$

- $d$ = dilation factor


> Instead of looking at every timestep, the model samples the past at different scales

This helps capture:
- short-term trends
- long-term regimes

---

### Gating mechanism

The model adds a gate:

$$  
h^{(temp)}_{i,t} = \tanh(W_f * x) \odot \sigma(W_g * x)  
$$

#### Interpretation
- $\tanh(\cdot)$ → candidate signal
- $\sigma(\cdot)$ → gate (importance)
- $\odot$ → element-wise multiplication

> “Filter useful temporal signals, suppress noise”

---

## Graph Attention Module (cross-asset relationships)

Now we model interactions between stocks.
At each time $t$, we have:

$$  
{h^{(temp)}_{1,t}, h^{(temp)}_{2,t}, \dots, h^{(temp)}_{N,t}}  
$$

##### Step 1: Linear projection
Each node is projected:

$$  
z_{i,t} = W h^{(temp)}_{i,t}  
$$
##### 2: Attention score
For stock $i$ attending to $j$:

$$  
e_{i,j,t} = a^\top [z_{i,t} ,|, z_{j,t}]  
$$
##### Step 3: Softmax normalization

$$  
\alpha_{i,j,t} = \frac{\exp(e_{i,j,t})}{\sum_{k} \exp(e_{i,k,t})}  
$$
##### Step 4: Aggregation

$$  
h^{(graph)}_{i,t} = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \alpha_{i,j,t} \cdot z_{j,t}  
$$
Intuition
- $z_{i,t}$ → representation of stock $i$
- $\alpha_{i,j,t}$ → how much $j$ influences $i$
- output = weighted combination of other stocks



Finally, both modules are fused:

$$  
h_{i,t} = h^{(temp)}_{i,t} + h^{(graph)}_{i,t}  
$$

(or concatenation in some implementations)


$$  
\text{FSTGAT} = \text{temporal patterns} + \text{learned market structure}  
$$
